## Section 6 - Debt Structure Analysis

Source: "Debt Profile - 30-04-2026.xlsx" (loan-account level), cross-checked to the audited/MIS borrowing notes. Total outstanding Rs 23.69 Cr as on 30-Apr-2026, reconciling to BS total borrowings of Rs 23.94 Cr at 31-Mar-2026 (LT 3.27 + ST 20.67) - the ~Rs 0.25 Cr difference is one month of amortisation/CC movement. No reconciliation flag.

**Lender profile: Green.** Three institutional lenders only - Federal Bank (private scheduled bank), Union Bank of India (PSU scheduled bank) and NSIC (National Small Industries Corporation, a central-government enterprise). No NBFCs, no AIFs, no private debentures (NCD/OCD) and no promoter/related-party inter-corporate deposits. Two scheduled banks plus a government institution is genuine credit validation; the absence of high-cost NBFC/AIF money is a positive sign that banks have not declined the name. Lender count 3; largest lender Federal Bank Rs 12.20 Cr = 51.5% of debt (moderate-high single-lender concentration); banks 84% (Federal 51.5% + Union 32.6%), government-FI 16% (NSIC). All three are well-known, credible institutions.

- Secured vs unsecured: effectively 100% secured / 0% unsecured. Weighted-average interest rate ~12.2% across the book (cash credit at 12.75% is the bulk). There is no unsecured tranche - all debt is collateralised by stock, book debts and promoter real estate. The blended ~12% cost is on the high side, so a lower-priced facility that displaces the dearer cash-credit lines would be margin-accretive in its own right.
- Related-party / promoter debt: none on the liability side - there are no director or related-company loans payable. The related-party exposure runs the other way: the company has lent Rs 6.58 Cr to a director (asset side; see Section 5). No quasi-equity reclassification applies.
- Encumbered FD / cash collateral: Rs 0.97 Cr of fixed deposits sit in other non-current assets, most likely margin money/lien against the working-capital limits - excluded from the Section 2 runway cash (which uses only the Rs 0.61 Cr free cash).
- Maturity / refinancing + FX: ~96% of debt is short-term revolving or rolling working-capital (cash credit, WCDL/WCTL); only ~4% is amortising term/vehicle debt. The book therefore depends heavily on annual renewal of bank limits - normal for a working-capital-intensive manufacturer with scheduled-bank lenders, but it does mean the business is structurally reliant on limit rollover, which is the same point that drives the thin runway in Section 2. All debt is rupee-denominated; there is no foreign-currency borrowing and hence no FX risk on servicing (the UAE receivable carries FX risk on the asset side only).
