## Section 0 - Company, Industry & Founder Overview

### About the Company

Popees is a Kerala-based babywear and baby-care brand that manufactures and sells infant and toddler garments, plus an expanding range of baby FMCG and accessories, to Indian (and some GCC) consumers through exclusive brand outlets, franchise stores, general-trade wholesale/distribution, and online marketplaces. It is vertically integrated: own and leased garment factories (Kerala, Karnataka, Tirupur) feeding a multi-channel retail network of ~105 stores plus marketplace and EBO sales. Revenue is predominantly product sales (transactional), part B2C through owned/franchise stores and part B2B through distributors and large-format retail.

| Vertical | What it is | % of revenue |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Baby & kids apparel | Core knitted/woven babywear, in-house manufactured; sold via EBOs, franchise, wholesale, online | Dominant (majority); not separately disclosed |
| Baby FMCG | Diapers, body lotion, bamboo wipes, soap, fabric wash; mostly third-party manufactured under Popees brand | Growing minority; n.a. |
| Accessories / supportives | Teethers, bottles, strollers, soft toys, hospital/maternity kits | Small; n.a. |

Business model: product sales, transactional, across a mix of company-owned-company-operated (COCO), franchise-invested-company-operated (FICO, 12% commission on net sales), FOCO and FOFO store formats, supplemented by distributor wholesale and e-commerce (Ajio, Amazon, Meesho). Stage: mature/scaling - ~Rs 162 Cr revenue (FY26 provisional), profitable, crossed the Rs 100 Cr turnover mark around 2021.

(Legal and ownership: standalone public limited company, recently converted from private limited; promoter Shaju Thomas holds 73.76%. Part of a 12-entity Popees Group, several merging into PBCPL over FY26-FY27. Standalone financials used; consolidated not provided. From company_structure.md.)

### About the Industry

India's baby and mother-care market (apparel plus baby FMCG/products) is large and structurally growing, supported by ~143 million children aged 0-6, rising disposable incomes, urbanisation, and a shift from unbranded to branded babywear. Organised penetration is still low, which is the growth thesis, but it is also a crowded value segment. (Market-size figures here are indicative from general knowledge and the company deck; to be confirmed with a sector report in diligence.)

Vintage: the Popees brand has operated for ~20 years (founded 2003, first factory 2005 per the deck), though the legal entity PBCPL dates to 2016 (~10 years). That is a long vintage for a regional babywear brand and implies real brand equity in South India, established manufacturing know-how, and survival through multiple demand cycles, including COVID. Resilience reads as moderate-to-strong regionally, less proven outside South India where the EBO roll-out is just beginning.

| Player archetype | Example(s) | Business model in one line |
|------------------|-----------|----------------------------|
| Organised baby-retail platforms | FirstCry (Brainbees, listed) | Omni-channel mother-and-baby marketplace plus private labels, national scale |
| Listed knitwear / apparel majors | Lux Industries, Rupa, Dollar Industries, Page Industries | Branded knitted garments, manufacturing-led, gross margins ~30-55% |
| Branded kidswear specialists | Gini & Jony, Hopscotch, Nino Bambino | Kidswear brands, mix of retail and online, mostly unlisted |
| Regional / unorganised | local babywear makers, FOCO/FOFO operators | Price-led, fragmented, limited brand, the segment Popees is consolidating |

The competitive reality woven through the read: this is a value-segment, trend-sensitive category where a handful of well-capitalised national players (FirstCry above all) and a long tail of unorganised makers both press on price, so the defensible edge has to come from in-house manufacturing cost control, brand trust, and store economics rather than pricing power.
