Our read: this is the central weakness. Popees is profitable on the P&L and is steadily de-levering (Total Debt Rs 38.1 Cr -> 29.5 Cr -> 23.9 Cr), but it does not throw off enough free cash to cover its own interest in cash terms - cash ICR is below 1x in all three years (0.85x, negative, 0.68x), and Debt/FCF sits above the 6x comfort line whenever FCF is positive. The reason is working capital: FY25 operations consumed Rs 39.7 Cr of cash (a Rs 13 Cr inventory build plus a Rs 36 Cr jump in advances to suppliers), funded almost entirely by a ~Rs 54 Cr equity raise. FY26 swings back to +Rs 6.6 Cr OCF as some of those advances unwind, but after Rs 3.2 Cr of store capex the free cash that remains (Rs 3.35 Cr) still does not fully cover Rs 4.96 Cr of interest. So debt today is being serviced out of fresh equity, the unwinding of working capital, and rolling bank limits rather than out of durable free cash flow. For a venture-debt facility this is the issue to structure around: the cash conversion has to improve (inventory and supplier-advance discipline) for the business to self-fund debt service. (DSCR is not computed - principal schedules are partial and with long-term principal assumed refinanced it collapses to ICR.)
