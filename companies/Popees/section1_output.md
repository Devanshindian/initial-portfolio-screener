## Section 1 - Preliminary Rejection Check

Section 1 - PASS (0 of 3 triggered)

Rule 1 (Deep Tech): Not triggered. Popees manufactures and sells baby garments and baby-care FMCG - a vertically-integrated consumer products business with ~Rs 162 Cr of predictable, recurring product revenue (FY26 provisional). No research/IP-stage product, no milestone or grant dependence. Not deep tech.

Rule 2 (Govt Revenue): Not triggered. Customers are private retailers, distributors, franchisees and marketplaces - the debtor ageing (31 Mar 2026) is dominated by names like BR Trading, Fashion Factory, V-Mart Retail, Future Lifestyle Fashions, Ajio, Amazon and Meesho, all private. No government, PSU, PSB or municipal client of any size was identified. Government revenue is effectively nil, far below the 30% threshold.

Rule 3 (Negative Unit Economics): Not triggered. On the FY26 provisional MIS, CM2 = Rs 46.86 Cr, 28.9% of revenue (Gross margin 33.4%, less CM1 logistics/commission costs to CM1 29.6%, less advertising/sales-promotion to CM2 28.9%). CM1 is firmly positive, so the company is not selling below cost. Unit economics are healthy. The MIS is annual only, so a quarterly CM2 trajectory cannot be computed - noted, but the full-period margin is clearly positive with no deterioration signal available to contradict it.

Outcome: PASS, 0 of 3 triggered. Proceed to Section 2.
