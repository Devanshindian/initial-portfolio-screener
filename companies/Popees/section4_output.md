### B. Key Findings

Unit economics are sound: FY26 CM2 of 28.9% means each rupee of sale comfortably covers materials, logistics, distributor commission and marketing before fixed costs. Margin trajectory is the strong part of the story - gross margin is flat at a respectable ~33-35%, but EBITDA margin has roughly doubled from 9.4% (FY24) to 18.5% (FY26 provisional) and PAT margin from 1.5% to 11.3%, because fixed costs are falling as a share of revenue as volume scales: employee cost 8.8% -> 5.9%, finance cost 5.8% -> 3.1%, with rent and overhead broadly contained. That is genuine operating leverage. Two cautions. First, the FY26 figures are provisional/unaudited and the implied step-up in profitability is large - it should be reconciled to audited FY26 numbers and to GST/sales data in diligence; the deck's own FY26 projection (Rs 170.4 Cr revenue, slightly different gross margin) does not exactly match the MIS, so the management numbers are not internally tight. Second, the P&L strength is not yet matched by cash (Section 3) - profit is real on accrual but is sitting in inventory, receivables and supplier advances rather than in the bank. Other income is immaterial (Rs 0.40 Cr, 0.2% of revenue), so profit is not being propped up by non-operating items. No large one-off was visible in FY26; FY25 carried a Rs 3.83 Cr bad-debt write-off within other expenses, a reminder that receivables quality has bitten before.
